At Drew, we value student voice. To create that voice, our learning culture requires trust,
respect, collaboration, and accountability. These values empower our school-wide learning
outcomes in pursuit of Creative and Critical Thinking and Integrity. As a community, we strive to
think critically about the validity of information, our own assumptions, and systemic bias.
Moreover, we honor and acknowledge the contributions and ideas that influence or support our
work and thinking. Guided by these values and learning outcomes, and against a background in
which AI is becoming increasingly ubiquitous in our lives, we have crafted the following AI
Policy.
This policy is intended to:
●​ Create opportunities for students to face challenges independently; it is when we are
challenged that we learn.
●​ Deepen our understanding of integrity.
●​ Foster trust by clarifying expectations about using AI, creating opportunities for
competence and confidence in our work, rather than fear of being caught or punished.
●​ Impart digital literacy by allowing students to discern the quality and truth of AI-created
content.
●​ Instill critical thinking skills by empowering students to consider the information they
consume.
Thus, we offer the following AI Policy:
Our baseline expectation is that students will work without the use of AI unless given explicit
permission and guidance from their teacher. The expectation that students will not use AI for
any purpose, including background research, learning support, editing, or content creation,
extends throughout the learning process.
In some instances, teachers will allow opportunities for student AI use that follow specific
parameters on discrete assignments. In these cases, AI use will be clearly outlined and
modeled by teachers. Students will use approved AI platforms, cite their use of AI, and share
documentation of their AI use with teachers. These instances will be guided by department
policies.

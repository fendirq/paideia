APPROVED SOURCE MATERIAL:

--- Source 1: al-Tabari packet excerpt ---
- al-Tabari describes support gathering in Khurasan under black banners and connects the movement to "the family of the Prophet."
- The excerpt emphasizes that Abbasid propaganda could unite different factions without spelling out one exact political program.
- The narrative suggests that eastern mobilization mattered because distance from Damascus gave the movement room to organize.

--- Source 2: Lecture packet on social grievances ---
- Mawali resentment mattered because conversion did not erase older hierarchies in taxation, military prestige, and political access.
- The packet notes that mawali grievance alone does not fully explain the revolution; coalition building and Umayyad factional weakness mattered too.
- One summary phrase used in the packet is "a coalition of grievances rather than a single class uprising."

--- Source 3: Seminar notes on the Battle of the Zab ---
- The Battle of the Zab in 750 was the decisive military defeat that ended Umayyad control in the central caliphate.
- The notes stress that the battle mattered because earlier political anger had finally been turned into coordinated force.

--- Source 4: Administrative and urban change under the early Abbasids ---
- Baghdad was founded in 762 under al-Mansur and became a capital tied to eastern trade routes and Persian administrative culture.
- Early Abbasid government expanded the role of secretaries and viziers and relied more visibly on Persian administrative practice.
- Seminar discussion frames this as evidence that the revolution changed the empire's center of gravity, not merely its ruling family.

--- Source 5: Historiographical note for comparison ---
- One modern interpretation in the packet argues that the revolution should not be reduced to mawali resentment because it depended on Umayyad factional crisis, eastern military organization, and the Abbasids' ability to speak in deliberately broad terms.
- Another interpretation argues that the revolution was social in effect even if it was not socially uniform in origin, because it restructured who could matter within the empire.

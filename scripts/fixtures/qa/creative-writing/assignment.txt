Assignment Prompt - Personal Narrative / Creative Nonfiction Essay

Write a 1100 to 1400 word creative nonfiction essay, with 1200 words as your target, that grows from lived experience rather than research. You may choose one of three broad directions:

- A personal narrative centered on a meaningful experience, turning point, or small moment that changed how you understand something
- A reflective piece about a formative relationship or a place that has shaped you
- A creative nonfiction essay that uses narrative techniques to explore an idea through scenes, objects, memory, and reflection

Your piece does not need to tell the "biggest" story of your life. In fact, essays are often stronger when they stay close to one vivid thread: an afternoon, a room, a routine, a conversation, a recurring object, or a place you know well. Let the essay discover meaning through attention rather than announcing meaning too early.

Required deliverable:
- One polished essay of 1100 to 1400 words
- A clear narrative or reflective arc, even if the essay moves through time nonlinearly

Craft expectations:
- Include concrete sensory detail so the reader can enter the world of the essay
- Build at least two full scenes with action, setting, and dialogue or quoted language when relevant
- Avoid relying only on summary; let key moments unfold on the page before you explain what they mean

You may draw lightly on the optional craft resources from class, but this is not a source-based argument. Your goal is to make the reader feel that the voice, perspective, and patterns of attention could only belong to this narrator.

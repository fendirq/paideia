Rubric - Personal Narrative / Creative Nonfiction Essay

Target length: 1200 words
Acceptable range: 1100 to 1400 words
Total: 100 points

1. Narrative Structure and Control of Time - 30 points
- The essay has a clear arc or organizing thread
- Scenes are arranged intentionally rather than randomly
- The writer moves between present action, memory, and reflection in ways that feel controlled
- An A-range piece knows when to slow down, when to skip forward, and how to land the ending without feeling abrupt

2. Scene Construction and Balance of Scene vs. Summary - 25 points
- The essay builds key moments as scenes rather than reporting everything from a distance
- Setting, action, gesture, and dialogue or quoted language are used purposefully
- Summary is used strategically to compress time, not to replace the most important moments
- Lower-scoring essays stay vague, rush pivotal moments, or explain what happened instead of dramatizing it

3. Voice, Language, and Sensory Detail - 30 points
- The voice feels specific to the narrator rather than generic or overly imitative
- Diction, syntax, rhythm, and imagery create a recognizable presence on the page
- Concrete sensory detail helps the reader inhabit the essay
- Strong essays take stylistic risks while remaining readable; weaker essays rely on generic language, filler reflection, or decorative but empty imagery

4. Meaning, Reflection, and Resonance - 15 points
- The essay does more than recount events; it discovers or complicates meaning
- Reflection grows organically from scenes instead of repeating obvious lessons
- The ending leaves the reader with an afterimage, question, or emotional echo
- Mechanical correctness matters, but grammar and punctuation are evaluated in relation to clarity and control, not as a separate category

Essays usually lose points for telling instead of showing, generic language, no sensory detail, and summary standing in for scene. The strongest essays make the reader trust the narrator's voice and feel that the meaning was earned on the page.

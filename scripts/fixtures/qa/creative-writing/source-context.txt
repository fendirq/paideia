OPTIONAL CRAFT AND CLASS CONTEXT:

--- Source 1: Sample student narratives discussed in workshop ---
- We discussed how effective student essays often stay with one manageable thread instead of trying to summarize an entire childhood or relationship.
- Several examples used a recurring object or repeated sound to create coherence.
- Class notes emphasized that reflection lands better after a scene has done some emotional work.

--- Source 2: Craft essay excerpt on scene construction ---
- The excerpt argues that a scene usually needs at least three things on the page at once: a body in motion, a physical setting, and some form of tension, even if the tension is quiet.
- It warns that summary can flatten emotional stakes when the writer skips over the very moment the reader most needs to witness.
- One line from discussion notes: "Stay in the room long enough for the meaning to arrive."

--- Source 3: Mini-lesson notes on reflection and endings ---
- Reflection should deepen or complicate the scene, not simply translate it into a moral.
- Endings in creative nonfiction often work by returning to an image, gesture, place, or line of dialogue with new pressure.
- We noted in class that sensory detail is not decoration; it is one of the main ways a narrator reveals attention, memory, and care.

Rubric - Comparative Essay 2: Frankenstein and Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde

Target length: 1500 words
Acceptable range: 1400 to 1600 words

Total: 100 points

1. Comparative Thesis and Argument - 25 points
- 10 points: Presents a specific, arguable thesis in the introduction rather than a topic statement or a list of similarities/differences
- 10 points: Uses comparison to answer the assignment's central question about how monstrosity is produced and what that comparison reveals
- 5 points: Takes a competing interpretation seriously and responds to it rather than dismissing it in one sentence

2. Use of Primary Textual Evidence - 25 points
- 10 points: Incorporates at least two relevant passages from Frankenstein and at least two relevant passages from Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde
- 10 points: Performs close reading that explains diction, narrative perspective, imagery, or structure instead of summarizing plot
- 5 points: Integrates quotations smoothly with attribution and explains why each quotation matters

3. Use of Source Packet and Primary/Secondary Comparison - 20 points
- 8 points: Accurately uses at least one analytical or secondary source from the packet
- 8 points: Explicitly compares a primary textual moment with a secondary claim instead of treating sources as separate boxes to check
- 4 points: Distinguishes clearly between what the literary text shows and how critics or lecture materials interpret it

4. Organization and Development - 15 points
- 6 points: Paragraphs are focused, each body paragraph advances the argument, and topic sentences do analytical work
- 5 points: Transitions make the comparison easy to follow and the paper builds in a logical sequence
- 4 points: The conclusion does more than repeat the thesis; it qualifies, extends, or clarifies the argument's stakes

5. Style, Mechanics, and Assignment Requirements - 15 points
- 5 points: Prose is clear, readable, and appropriate for an undergraduate literary analysis
- 5 points: Sentence-level control is strong overall, with few errors in grammar, punctuation, or citation framing
- 5 points: Meets formal requirements, including the 1400 to 1600 word range, comparative focus, and use of both primary and secondary material

An A-range essay for this assignment will make an argument that could not be reduced to "both texts show monstrosity." It will use evidence selectively, compare rather than alternate between summaries, and show why the difference between Shelley's and Stevenson's models of monstrosity matters. Essays usually lose ground when they become descriptive, rely on vague claims about "society" or "human nature," or mention criticism without actually putting it into conversation with the primary texts.

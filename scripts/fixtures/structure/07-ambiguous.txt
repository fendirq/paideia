US History — Chapter 12 Notes (personal)

- Civil War kicks off after Lincoln's election, Nov 1860. South sees it as existential threat re: slavery.
- Fort Sumter: April 12, 1861. Confederates fire first. That's the moment, technically.
- Anaconda Plan vs. Scott's strategy: blockade the coast + take the Mississippi. Slow but eventually worked.
- Key battles to remember: First Bull Run ('61, Confederate win, showed the war wouldn't be short), Antietam ('62, bloodiest single day, "win" for Union = pretext for Emancipation Proc), Gettysburg ('63, turning point), Vicksburg ('63, splits Confederacy).
- Emancipation Proclamation: Jan 1, 1863. Didn't actually free anyone immediately (only applied to Confederate states still in rebellion) but reframed the war morally and kept Britain from siding with South.
- Sherman's March to the Sea — Nov-Dec '64. Total war concept. Effective but brutal.
- Lee surrenders at Appomattox Apr 9 1865. Lincoln assassinated Apr 14.
